Description

A smash burger is similar to a bruger but the difference lies in the patties,
as smash patties are smashed on the grill in order to sear them more, thus giving
a distinct flavor!
Let's begin!

Original recipe here!

Ingredients

  • Ground beef
  • Cheese
  • Buns
  • Toppings of your desire
  • Any sauce you want

Steps

  1. Prep the meat. Allot 4 oz (113g) per burger. Be very gentle and just loosely form
    the meat into round balls. Sprinkle salt and pepper on the tops of each ball. Pop this
    in the fridge or freezer while you start heating the pan
  2. Lightly oil a cast iron pan and heat over medium-high heat until smoking. It is
    essential that the pan be very hot/smoking. Add a ball of the chilled and seasoned
    ground beef to the pan, seasoned side down, then immediately press straight down until flat.
  3. Cook until a crust forms (approximately 1 minute, don't cook either side too long or you'll
    make the burgers tough). While this cooks, sprinkle the surface of the patty with additional
    salt and pepper. Next, use a spatula to scrape up the burger and flip. Make sure you scrape
    that caramelized crust with the patty!
  4. Cook the burger until done, about 45 seconds or so, before transferring to a clean plate. If you'd
    like, you can add a slice of cheese here, too, so it will start melting. Then transfer the burger
    to a plate and cover with foil to keep warm.
  5. Toast the buns. Add butter to the hot pan, and, once melted, turn off the pan and add your buns
    cut-side down. Cook until the buns are warm and toasted (this takes only seconds), then serve!
